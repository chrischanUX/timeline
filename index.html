One year has passed,

and each day has been an incredible adventure with you.

Here are some of my favorite ones.

After I found out how much you loved camping, I took you to my favorite spot in the world. We slept under the stars, trying to keep warm next to a fire (with Rebecca). We only slept a few hours and talked all night long.

On our first of many trips to LA, we wandered around IKEA eating frozen yogurt, hung out with Kristie having good Chinese food, and as night came, we fell asleep in my car on the side of a road in the mountains.

We camped in the freezing cold of Sequoia National Park, waking up to snow all around us. We made hot soup to keep us warm as we explored Kings Canyon the next day.

We drove to SF and brought Cody back to SB for a couple of weeks. He bonded with Penny and I could tell how much he enjoyed SB.

We stayed in SF for a week, where we went camping in Samuel Taylor Park, explored the city, and ate lots.

When you were living in LA over the summer, I visited you for a weekend and we stayed in our own little "treehouse."

With the ugly couple (Tom and Siri), we drove down to LA and had dim sum, explored Japantown, strolled around the Grove, and beat an escape room.

After stuffing ourselves with too much McConnell's, we decided to get our own ice cream maker. We spent nights making salted caramel ice cream and Thai tea ice cream. I think they were almost better than McConnell's.

I surprised you while we were in Berkeley by taking you to a concert by our favorite band, The Lumineers. At one point they were only a few feet from us, and I think we both lost our shit.

We drove into the desert and backpacked a few miles away from the road, eventually finding our own big rock (with a Joshua Tree on it) to camp on.

During my surgery, anesthesia made me one hell of an asshole. Despite that, you took care of me, cooked food for me, and went around town to buy me smoothies,

Moving out of Santa Barbara wasn't the easiest thing to do. We left a lot of great friends and memories behind. But coming to the Bay Area and spending the holidays with my mom and Cody with you was the best time ever.

Long distance sucks. But you've always been supportive of my travels, and I'm so thankful for that. Thanks for putting up with me wanting to go out and see the world. I can't wait to be back home with you.
